Afang

Afang soup

Afang is a vegetable soup that originates from the Efik people of the Efik kingdom in Cross River State and the Ibibio People of Akwa Ibom in Southern Nigeria.

It It is rich in vegetables,meats and palm oil. The originators of this dish love to add a lot of seafood to it as well.

Ingredients

  • 15 cups Sliced fresh waterleaves
  • 1/2 cup ground crayfish
  • 3 cups Ground fresh Ukazi leaves
  • 3 cubes knorr
  • 2 medium-sized dried or roasted fish
  • Soft cow skin (pomo)
  • Goat meat (optional)
  • 1.5 cups palm oil
  • 1 cup Periwinkles (optional)
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 6 Red fresh pepper
  • 1 KG Assorted meat
  • Stockfish

Cooking instruction

  1. Grind the ukazi leaves, it acts as the thickener for this soup. Season the meat with a half cup of sliced onions, salt, two seasoning cubes and other spice of choice) Slice the water leaves and set aside in a sieve so excess water will drain away.
  2. Wash the stockfish, dried fish and add them to the cooking meat, let them cook till they are soft and the pot is almost dry. Add the palm oil, crayfish and stir all together. Add one seasoning cube.
  3. Allow to simmer for 6 minutes while you stir continuously. Add the water-leaves.
  4. Ground pepper, snails and salt to taste, allow cooking for 3-5 minutes. Add the ground ukazi leaves.
  5. Stir all together, cover the pot and allow another 5 minutes, stirring every 2 minutes.
